Southern Biscuits

Description

Biscuits are different baked goods, depending on which side of the Atlantic ocean you are on. The North American biscuit would be similar to a scone in the UK, but with a fluffier crumb. They both evolved from hard tack, which was originally made with only flour and water.

In the south-eastern US, wheat would typically have less protein. This lead to flour that was easier to use in quick breads, like the biscuit. Prior to national food supply chains, across New England and the Midwest only high-protein flours were available.

Today, modern recipes typically include chemical leaveners such as baking powder and baking soda, and a shortener such as lard or butter. As well, its typical to see either milk or buttermilk. They are typically rolled out and cut into shape.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups AP flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 8 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup whole milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425Â°F
  2. In a bowl combine dry ingredients
  3. Cube butter and add to dry ingredients
  4. Mix butter with hands or fork until a cornmeal consistency is achieved
  5. Add milk and mix until homogeneous
  6. Roll biscuits out to approx. 3/4 of an inch thick
  7. Use a mug, cup, or cookie cutter to cut out rounds of dough
  8. Arrange rounds in a cast iron pan
  9. Bake for 12 to 15 minutes
  10. Remove from oven and eat hot
