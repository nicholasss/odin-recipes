Chicory Coffee

Description

Native to europe, chicory has long been cultivated for its root as a non-caffeinated substitute for coffee. As well, the leaves of the plant are utilized for salads or in cooked meals.

It is not known when chicory root began being used as a coffee additive, however it is known to be used all over the world. Within the US, it is associated with New Orleans, LA and Caf√© du Monde in particular.

Prep time: 10 mintues
Brew time: 5 mintues
Servings: 1 Mug

Ingredients

  • 30 grams of ground chicory
  • 40 grams of ground French Roast coffee
  • 4 cups of water

Instructions

  1. Heat 4 cups of water to ~200 degrees fahrenheit.
  2. Add the coffee and chicory to the french press.
  3. Pour in enough water to cover the grounds and stir it with a spoon. Let it sit for one minute.
  4. After waiting for one minute, add the remaining water to the french press, and stir once more. Let it sit for four minutes.
  5. After brewing, place the top of the french press on, and slowly press the pluger down.
  6. Pour into your favorite mug and enjoy!
